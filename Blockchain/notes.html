Morpho

NEAR Protocol

This is a community friendly and Python friendly layer 1 chain, the most interesting thing for me is, it includes decentralized AI projects such as train AI with homomorphic encryption, which is just supported in 2025 by NVIDIA, and a project that contributors can train AI agents to make transactions.

BNB Chain

This is a layer 1 chain that claims to focus on AI solutions, offering high throughput and large data storage. It also holds hackathons, and participating in them can help you gain skills and prestige in the industry. It has a retailer-friendly environment. Maybe I’ll be more interested once I look into it further…

Solana

It aims to be fast and scalable, but it is not more decentralized than Ethereum. The community has very active memecoin culture (oh, you think $TRUMP isn’t a memecoin? Of course it is). Their marketing strategy feels much more like real-world consumer marketing — just look at their phone and the airdrops…

Polygon

Aims to scale Ethereum. It offers multiple solutions—sidechains, zk-rollups, and other L2 technologies—to increase throughput while inheriting Ethereum’s security. Its ecosystem is heavily focused on developer friendliness, partnerships, and mainstream adoption (think Starbucks, Reddit, Nike). The trade-off is that it relies heavily on Ethereum’s base layer and has been criticized for centralization in some of its earlier chains (like Polygon PoS). Still, its branding and business development strategies are strong, positioning it as the “gateway” for bringing Web2 companies into Web3.

Video Game

This category is highly hyped. I’ve tried several projects, including the once-popular AAA game Illuvium on Epic. It offers only the bare minimum of enjoyment while relying heavily on grinding mechanics. Most players are motivated by earning money, not by having fun. Web3 doesn’t provide any real solutions for the gaming industry, which is already losing its playful and exploratory spirit.
