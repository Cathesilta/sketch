Motivation

The ability of embodiment (具現化) has always been very intriguing to me; it means having the superpower to conjure useful objects out of thin air.

I found that investing has a lot to do with this ability. It’s about using knowledge to see wealth that others cannot see.

Ethical

I pursue long-term investment. As a retail investor, it is either foolish or overly confident to compete in a battleground dominated by institutions through frequent monthly investing. Considering the Gambler’s Ruin principle and the fact that retail investors have less information than whales or institutions, the short-term market is infernal for us. An interesting exception is that I have a friend, a retail investor, who has successfully won the game with his short-term investment skills (and continues to make a profit today).

However, I still find myself more suited to long-term strategies.

I almost never buy in like a speculator (driven by FOMO), thanks to my clear goal (I can specify the exact amount of money I want to earn) and my self-discipline.

I remain calm and act with agility, especially in dangerous situations. I see risk as a worthy opponent, which is why I enjoy this game.

I almost always think in terms of game theory, like spontaneously. This is something that exists in every high-level battlefield I’ve seen.

Learner

I'm still very fresh but enthusiastic about this role.

My perspective on being an investor is a pilgrimage of self-exploration and self-mastery.
